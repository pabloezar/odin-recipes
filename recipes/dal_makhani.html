Dal Makhani

Description

Dal Makhani is a classic Indian dish made with whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma) slow-cooked to creamy perfection in a rich, buttery, and smoky tomato-based gravy. It's known for its luxurious texture and deep flavors, often finished with a swirl of cream and fresh cilantro.

This comforting and hearty vegetarian main is a staple in North Indian cuisine and a must-try for anyone exploring Indian flavors. It pairs wonderfully with naan bread, rice, or roti.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal), washed and soaked overnight
  • 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma), washed and soaked overnight
  • 4 cups water (for pressure cooking)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup tomato puree (from 2-3 ripe tomatoes)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream (or coconut cream for dairy-free)
  • 2 tbsp butter (or vegan butter for dairy-free)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • For Tempering (optional but recommended for smoky flavor)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Steps

  1. Cook the Lentils: Drain the soaked black lentils and kidney beans. In a pressure cooker, combine them with 4 cups of fresh water. Cook on high pressure for 6-8 whistles (or about 25-30 minutes after the first whistle) until very soft and mushy. If not using a pressure cooker, boil them in a pot with plenty of water until tender, which may take 1-2 hours. Mash some of the lentils lightly with the back of a spoon for a creamier texture.
  2. Prepare the Gravy Base: Heat ghee or oil in a large heavy-bottomed pan or pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until golden brown, about 8-10 minutes. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute until the raw aroma disappears.
  3. Add Spices and Tomatoes: Add the tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates from the masala and the mixture thickens (about 8-10 minutes).
  4. Combine and Simmer: Add the cooked lentils and kidney beans to the gravy base. Add a little hot water if the dal is too thick, aiming for a creamy but flowing consistency. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook on low heat for at least 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld. The longer it simmers, the better it tastes.
  5. Finish the Dal: Stir in the garam masala, fresh cream, and butter. Mix well until everything is combined and heated through. Do not boil vigorously after adding cream.
  6. Prepare the Tempering (Tadka - Optional):** In a small pan, heat 1 tbsp ghee or oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds; once they splutter, add dried red chilies and asafoetida. Cook for a few seconds until fragrant. Pour this tempering directly over the Dal Makhani.
  7. Serve: Garnish generously with fresh chopped cilantro. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
